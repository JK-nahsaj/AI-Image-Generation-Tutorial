Enhance Learning with AI-Generated Images

A tutorial for Instructors on using AI visuals to engage students

->Why Use AI-Generated Images in the Classroom?

Modern education is shifting toward visual learning. With shorter attention spans and diverse learning needs, images help students grasp concepts faster and retain information longer. AI-generated images give instructors a flexible, engaging way to keep lessons fresh and visual.



Instructor using AI-generated resources

  • ✅ Save time generating lesson visuals
  • ✅ Customize images to match topics or level
  • ✅ Capture attention with rich visuals

Students engaging with AI-generated visuals

  • 🎯 Understand complex concepts visually
  • 🎨 Encourage imagination and creativity
  • 😄 Make lessons interactive and fun

->How to Generate AI Images Using ChatGPT+DALL·E

  1. Go to chat.openai.com and log in with your account.
  2. Start a new chat and select DALL·E from the options (usually labeled as "Image generation").
  3. In the message box, type a detailed image prompt. Example: “Generate an image showing perfect balance with using AI to enhance teaching and not replacing teaching with AI”
  4. Wait a few seconds while ChatGPT generates the image. You’ll see the image appear below your prompt.
  5. Click the image to enlarge or download it for your class use.
  6. Save the image or copy the link to embed it into your lesson content.







->Tips for Effective Use & Ethics

  • ✅ Be specific with your prompts: Detailed descriptions yield better image results. Try adding style, setting, and action.
  • ✅ Use clear subject references: Mention people, objects, and environments precisely (e.g., “A scientist in a lab, comic book style”).
  • ⚠️ Respect ethical boundaries: Avoid prompts that could generate misinformation, bias, or inappropriate content.
  • ⚠️ Fact-check content: Generated visuals may not always be accurate. Verify important visuals before using them in class.
  • ✅ Involve students: Encourage learners to write their own prompts for creative assignments or storytelling activities.
  • ✅ Promote visual literacy: Discuss how images are created, their purposes, and how to interpret them critically.
